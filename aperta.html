Project Overview

Challenge:

The intended user typically spends around 2 to 5 hours packing their equipment for various photography jobs they need to attend. On top of that, they invest additional hours in shopping for materials to restock their inventory. These tasks eat into their available time, preventing them from engaging in other activities. Moreover, packing becomes a source of stress when they have to keep track of everything that needs to be packed solely in their mind. Without a checklist, it's easy for the user to forget to pack certain items or even leave them behind at the job site.

Our Solution:

To provide the user with a stress-free and organized experience when packing and preparing equipment for a photo job, and to cut down on the time spent on this task and shopping for materials, allowing the user to dedicate more time to other activities.

Target Audience:

Professional Photographers

Understanding the User

User Research:

I conducted research through interviews with the targeted users. I observed all they said, thought, did, and felt and utilized the information provided to create an empathy map that helped determine the user's pain points. I then used their pain points to develop personas that had the same problems as my interviewees. Because I consider myself the type of person this app and interview were for, I had some assumptions about the interview responses. These assumptions were correct, as the interviewees said they felt stressed and overwhelmed when packing equipment and would like to reduce the time spend completing this task.

User Pain Points

1

Materials Run Out:

Photographers need materials and equipment that need to be repurchased often. Shopping for these items can take a long time out of their day, which they could utilize for other tasks.

2

Forgotten Equipment:

Users are unorganized and forgetful when they have to keep track of the equipment they pack in their heads, so as a result, no matter how responsible they are, they have a greater chance of losing equipment.

3

Overwhelmed:

Photographers usually plan what to pack in their head or on a paper checklist. Unfortunately, this makes packing difficult for the user considering the various different equipment needed for each job.

4

Time Packing:

Most photographers take about 2-5 hours to charge, pack, and prepare all the equipment needed for a photo job. The time used for packing could be spent on editing photos or other personal activities.

Starting the Design

Ideation:

To start off the design process, I began sketching down ideas, on pen and paper, for pages of our interface in which we could include every one of the app's intended features while also incorporating solutions to our user pain points.

The paper wire-frames were transferred into Figma to create the initial digital wire-frames. These wire-frames served as the foundation for our low-fidelity prototypes, which I will later show your how they were used for user testing and iteration.

Study Findings

Button Labeling:

Users have difficulty figuring out where the “inventory” button will lead them.

User Flow:

Users need help knowing what to do after their order is complete.

Add to Cart Button:

Users are unsure if their items were added to their cart since there is no confirmation button, animation, or sign of change.

Refining the Design

After conducting a usability study on our low-fidelity prototype, our reviewers found a few areas for improvement in this design. For example, there was no way to add items to our cart. Additionally, some reviewers suggested indicating which store offers the best price would be beneficial.

Going Forward

Takeaways

What We Learned:

While creating the first user flow of the app, Apertura, I learned that although attention to detail is an incredible skill to have, I have to remember to manage my time more wisely. I tend to spend too much time on the simplest details that probably don’t deserve that attention.

Impact:

Apertura is an app that could truly help photographers manage their time better and help them feel more stress free while preparing for the job.

A quote from our usability study:
“I love the way I can compare prices from different stores on this item. That will for sure come in handy, and after I can just press complete order!”
